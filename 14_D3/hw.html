Correlation Between Poverty and Healthcare Coverage

Healthcare and poverty levels are inextricably linked. This is shown in the above scatterplot where there is a positive correlation between healthcare percentage and poverty percentage in the data frame 2014's one-year estimates from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey. The calculated correlation is 0.51.

As the percentage of a state's population living in poverty increases, the percentage of the population without healthcare increases.
